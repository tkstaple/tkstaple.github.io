cEnergi Website

Website redesign for cEnergi, a research group aiming to transform consumer energy interfaces.


[ Explore the website ]
  • Design Research

Purpose

The cEnergi group needed a new website design that would encompass their transdisciplinary approach, lab various target audiences, and showcase their projects, collaborations, and research. Furthermore, because cEnergi is in between an academic and private research lab, the website's design also needed to illustrate this aspect.

Background

Consumer Energy Interfaces (cEnergi) investigates the potential for eco-feedback to promote resilient and sustainable relationships between individuals, communities, and natural resources. Eco-feedback is broadly defined as information about individual or group behavior related to natural resource (water, energy, food) processes (generation, storage, waste) provided back to that individual or group.

Content

There are three distinct target audiences the website needed to address: funders to privately fund the group, researchers to collaborate and establish connections with, and students to grow the team.

I conducted a number of interviews with random students, half the principle investigators of cEnergi, and other administration and staff. After understanding what the most important aspects of the website were, I created user personas for each of the three target audience groups.

The most common words interviewees used when describing the ideal cEnergi website included: "simple, practical, aesthetic, eye-catching, interesting, solving a problem, unique, interdisciplinary, academic, making an impact"

Flowchart

Design

Because cEnergi is in between an academic group and a startup, I wanted to reflect both ends of the spectrum visually. The large grey background images are of the lab space itself and were designed to show the depth and collaborative aspect of the group on every page. The Project page is the most important aspect of the website, since by seeing the breadth of cEnergi's projects, funders would be more interested to fund, researchers more interested in collaborating, and students more interested in getting involved.

Homepage

The landing page plays a timelapse of the cEnergi team working in the lab space which shows how the group is collaborative, synergetic, and energetic. The looping video with overlaid text emulates a classic startup website, but once the user enters to the rest of the site, the aesthetic changes to a more academic design.

Projects

Everyone's contribution matters, so I wanted to individually include and credit each contributing member on the project's team, which is directly linked to the People page. When a project is clicked, a lightbox appears with more in-depth information; a fixed custom-made filtering system also categorizes projects on a more macro level.

People

The makeup of cEnergi can be seen on this page, ranging all the way from the P.I.s to each contributing student involved on the group's projects. This provides transparency and shows the extent to which students are involved—showing cEnergi's multi-level as well as multidisciplinary aspect.
