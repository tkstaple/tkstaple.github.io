cEnergi Website

Website redesign for cEnergi, a research group aiming to transform consumer energy interfaces.


[ Explore the website ]
  • Design Research
  • Web Design
  • Student Work
2015-2016

Purpose

To redesign cEnergi's website to 1) show their trans-disciplinary approach, 2) showcase their research projects, and 3) cater the group to three distinct target audiences.

Context

Consumer Energy Interfaces (cEnergi) investigates the potential for eco-feedback to promote resilient and sustainable relationships between individuals, communities, and natural resources. Cenergi approaches eco-feedback research with a trans-disciplinary perspective, integrating behavior science, design, computer science, and engineering.

The group has a multitude of research projects that aim to transform consumer energy use through different interfaces including mobile, web, and even tangible.

Research

There were three distinct target audiences the website needed to address: 1) funders to privately fund the group, 2) researchers to collaborate and establish connections with, and 3) students to grow the team.

Interviews

I conducted a number of interviews with a number of students, half of cEnergi's principal investigators, and other cEnergi-affiliated administration and staff. The most common words interviewees used when describing the ideal cEnergi website included: "simple, practical, aesthetic, eye-catching, interesting, solving a problem, unique, interdisciplinary, academic, making an impact."

Personas

After hearing what everyone had to say about their vision of the website, I created three user personas for each of the three distinct target audience groups. Both funder and researcher persona information were pulled from interviews, while the student persona information is from the UC Davis Student Profile [ PDF ].

Existing Websites

Because cEnergi is technically in between an academic group and a startup/creative group, I wanted to reflect both ends of the spectrum in the website (these two are opposites in my opinion, since startup websites are structurally straightforward while academic websites are notoriously . . . well, unintuitive).

Content

The Project page is the most important part of the website; by seeing the diversity of cEnergi's projects, funders would be more interested in funding, researchers more interested in collaborating, and students more interested in getting involved. By linking Projects to People and vice-versa, the website generally leads to the Projects page, which is the main goal. Info Architecture

Design

Homepage

The landing page plays a timelapse of the cEnergi team working in the lab space. In interviews, a recurring goal brought up was to have visitors understand the atmosphere of the space—collaborative, synergetic, and energetic. Also, the looping HTML5 video with overlaid text is akin to the "classic" startup website.

Projects

Everyone's contribution matters.
It was important to both students and researchers to give credit where credit was due. Including everyone on the individual projects gives more incentive to students to join cEnergi, seeing as their contribution is transparent.
The custom filtering sidebar makes it easier for visitors to understand and find the kinds of projects they are looking for.

People

Who is cEnergi? Again, understanding who makes up the group is important for all three audiences. This page shows the range of people involved, from P.I.s to individual undergrads. This provides transparency into cEnergi's multi-level as well as multidisciplinary approach.

Concluding Thoughts

I learned an immense amount about user research and the steps necessary to gain enough insight to move on with a design. Although I conducted thorough research and applied it to the design process effectively, I am not fully happy about the actual development/end result of the website—developing the website from scratch by myself was not the best choice in hindsight. Then again, had I not coded the website myself, I would not have learned the HTML, CSS, & Javascript/jQuery that help me today in my other work.
